Year Fourteen

Our book. Four chapters. One day.

You'll find four riddles on this trail. Each one points to a real place in Brevard and Asheville. Read carefully — they don't hand you the answer. When you think you've found the spot, tap "We're Here" and the chapter unfolds. I'm with you the whole way.

Chapter One

Where It Started

The Riddle

The subject was woods and water, but I stayed for the face.
Back row, closer than required in Moore's first-floor space.
Find the room where two strangers became something else.

Chapter One

Moore Science 202

Fall 2000. You walked in already knowing how to make me laugh, and I spent a semester pretending the rivers were that interesting. Twenty-six years later I can tell you the rivers were not that interesting. You were. This is Chapter One, and the book reads forward from here. Press play. Then take my hand — we're not driving anywhere. We're walking.

The Walk

You know this walk. Science to music, every class day, you splitting off toward art halfway across. We did it for years before either of us called it anything. So let's do it once more — together this time, out loud about it. I'll shut up and let the path do the talking. Don't open the next chapter until our feet stop.

Chapter One, Beat Two

Where I Asked

Arrival

Here. The bench is gone but the spot isn't. This is where I asked you to write the rest of it with me. I brought provisions for the road — look for them first, you'll know them. Then look again. There's a second thing, and it's quieter.

The Box

Remember this parking lot? Two months before I got down on one knee, I handed you a box of cereal in your car right about here and let you dig a promise ring out of the marshmallows. It was clumsy and I knew it even then — you deserved better packaging than a cereal box. So this time it's in a real box. Same marshmallows, though. Some things shouldn't change.

The Scarf & The Blue

Don't look at it — smell it. You left this in my car after Serre's bonfire in 2010, and here's the part I never told you, all these years standing right next to you: it wasn't the perfume. It was the perfume and you, mixed into something I didn't have a word for — warm, and steadying, and the closest thing to home I'd felt in a long time. I kept the scarf on purpose. I wanted to keep the way it made the world feel safe. That was before we were brave. So I'm telling you now, out loud, with you close enough to hear it.

Chapter Two

The Long Bet

The Riddle

What's here now is not what was here then. Picture it before the books moved in — open ground, a diamond, the smell of a fair. We sat here once over a milkshake from Rocky's and made a deal so ridiculous we shook on it like it was binding.

Chapter Two

The Grounds

There it is. Before this was a library it was a field, and before we were anything sure we sat right around here and made that absurd pact — if we both struck out by some far-off year, we'd just end up together, like it was a fallback plan instead of the whole point. We were joking. We were also completely serious, the way you only are at that age. We didn't need the deadline. Press play.

Chapter Three

Two Strangers at a Bar

The Riddle

Two strangers at a bar, afraid to risk the friend,
Then one talked fruit so oddly that we had to play pretend.
Find the shop next to the coffee, where Kristen prints with care,
Ask for what I'm holding — it's waiting, it's there.

Chapter Three

Kristen's Place

This is where it almost ended and instead it started. Your thirty-day trial was about to run out and I knew it, so I did the only thing I know how to do — I made it weird. Two strangers at a bar, and me asking what you liked about a pineapple like it was the most important question in the world. You came back from the bathroom, sat down, and played along. That was the night I stopped being afraid of it. The thing I'm having held for you is yours — and it's pointing north. We're not done. Press play, then we drive.

Finale

Year Fourteen

Thirteen chapters. Moore Science, the field, the bench, the bar, and every ordinary Tuesday in between that never got a clue because there are too many to count. That's the book so far. You've read all of it today, in order, on foot and on the road. There's one chapter left, and it's the only one I couldn't write — because it's not mine to write alone. Here.

The Map

This is Year Fourteen. It's blank on purpose. There's a studio on it where you'll paint, and a room where I'll draw maps, and a lot of space I left empty because I don't get to decide what goes there — we do. Wherever the next house is, whatever we put in it, this is the only chapter I want to write with my whole life. Same deal as the field, except this time there's no deadline and no fallback. Just you, and the rest of it. Happy almost-anniversary, Joy. Let's go fill in the blank.
